Cats in Short

Cats are small, domesticated carnivorous mammals known for companionship and hunting vermin
They have a flexible body, sharp claws, and well-developed senses, and are known for sleeping
12-18 hours a day. The cat, also referred to as the domestic cat or house cat, It is the only
domesticated species of the family Felidae. Advances in archaeology and genetics have shown that
the domestication of the cat occurred in the Near East around 7500 BC.



Cats Behavior

Common cat behaviors include grooming, sleeping, and playing (often mimicking hunting).
Other frequent actions involve vocalizations like meowing and purring, body language
such as rubbing (bunting), kneading, and tail movements, and climbing or seeking high places.
Cats also communicate through facial expressions and ear positions, and their activity levels are
often highest at dawn and dusk.
There are many different behaviors of cats some of them you can see at list below.


Some social and communication behaviors of cats

  • Purring: Usually a sign of contentment, but can also occur under stress.
  • Bunting: Rubbing their cheeks on objects and people to mark territory with scent glands.
  • Kneading: A rhythmic pushing motion with their paws on soft surfaces, which is a remnant of kittenhood and often a sign of comfort.
  • Vocalizations: Meowing, chirping, yowling, and hissing are all normal forms of communication.
  • Slow blinks: A sign of trust and affection, similar to a feline kiss.
